Strategic Goals

  • Stop Leakage Eliminate geographic and audience targeting leakage draining ad spend
  • Attribution Build a measurable conversion tracking system tied to real lead events
  • Scalability Create a scalable campaign template replicable across future product lines

The Challenge

Most local ad campaigns fail the same way: broad targeting, vague conversion goals, and no reliable data connecting spend to outcome. Without knowing exactly which keywords, demographics, or placements are driving real conversions — versus clicks that go nowhere — campaign optimization becomes guesswork.

The core problem is not the ad platform. It's the absence of a structured targeting and tracking architecture before the budget is switched on. Money gets spent. Data gets collected. But the two are never properly connected.

The Strategy

The framework was designed around three principles: target precisely, track completely, and optimize with evidence.

  • Geo-targeted campaign structure: Campaigns were built with tight geographic filters centered on high-intent local segments — excluding broad reach areas that historically produced clicks without conversions.
  • Keyword architecture: Search campaigns were organized by intent tier — separating high-intent transactional keywords from informational ones, and bidding accordingly.
  • GA4 event mapping: Every meaningful user action — form submission, button click, page depth, time on site — was mapped as a GA4 event. This gave the campaign a real conversion signal rather than relying on pageview data.
  • HubSpot CRM integration: Qualified leads generated by the campaign were auto-fed into HubSpot, enabling follow-up tracking and closing-rate measurement that connected ad spend to actual business outcomes.

The Execution

The campaign structure was built before a single dollar of ad spend was committed. The targeting framework, conversion tracking, and CRM pipeline were all live and tested first — ensuring that when the budget was activated, every click had a measurable destination.

Search campaigns were segmented by keyword intent. Display and social campaigns on Meta were segmented by demographic and behavioral profile, with lookalike audiences built from existing high-value customer data.

Weekly performance reviews were built into the operational cadence — with a standardized GA4 reporting dashboard tracking cost-per-lead, conversion rate by segment, and CRM lead quality scores.

Impact & Outcomes

The framework is structured to deliver approximately 25% reduction in wasted ad spend by eliminating broad-match leakage, low-intent traffic, and untracked conversion paths.

More importantly, the system is replicable. Every campaign element — the targeting template, tracking setup, and CRM pipeline — is documented and transferable to new markets or product categories without rebuilding from scratch.
